Key Finding: Streak Mitigation is Unnecessary with ML Soft Scaling

ML soft-signal strategies (E: XGBoost, F: LightGBM) continuously scale position sizes based on predicted trade quality (0.3x–1.2x on every trade), making the reactive streak mitigation rules (reduce risk after 3/6/9 consecutive losses) redundant. 4. Tiered Compounding: Baseline (with Streak) vs E/F (no Streak)

Why Streak Mitigation is Removed for E and F

The ML soft scaling already continuously dampens risk on every trade: E averages 0.62x scale, F averages 0.65x. Stacking streak mitigation on top compounds the risk reduction excessively (e.g., streak ×0.60 × ML ×0.62 = 0.37x total), sacrificing returns without proportional drawdown benefit. The ML scaling alone provides better risk-adjusted performance.

  • Maintain tiered risk + streak mitigation on top of ML scaling
  • Updated: Remove streak mitigation when using ML soft scaling
