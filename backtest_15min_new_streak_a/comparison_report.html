Filter Effectiveness Analysis

How the Adaptive Filters Work

ADX Filter: Blocks entries when ADX < 20 (ranging/choppy market conditions). The ADX (Average Directional Index) measures trend strength. Lower ADX indicates lack of trend, which historically leads to whipsaw losses.

Progressive Positioning Threshold: After consecutive losses, the required positioning signal strength increases:

  • 0 losses: pos_long > 0.4 required
  • 1 loss: pos_long > 0.5 required
  • 2 losses: pos_long > 0.6 required
  • 3 losses: pos_long > 0.8 required
  • 4+ losses: pos_long > 1.0 required (effectively blocked)

Cooldown Period: After 5+ consecutive losses, a 24-hour (96 bars) cooldown is enforced.

Key Findings

  • ADX Filter Impact: Blocks ~27,500 potential entries in ranging markets. Most effective when combined with progressive positioning.
  • Progressive Positioning Impact: Blocks 10,000-14,500 entries that don't meet threshold requirements after losses.
  • Combined Effect: Adaptive_Conservative achieves ZERO 6+ losing streaks while maintaining 353% return.
  • Trade-off: More aggressive filtering reduces total trades but significantly improves quality.
